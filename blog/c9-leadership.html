Leadership

What is your experience of compassionate leadership?

When I was at university, my mother was diagnosed with breast cancer. I had an exam and couple of assignments due in the days following, which I found very difficult to prioritize. The faculty heads showed a massive amount of compassion, giving me extensions on the assignments and increasing my grade for the exam, which I still sat on the original date.

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

I rather enjoyed this one actually. It felt liberating to think of everyone as good people, especially the few who I don't usually think of in that light.

How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? How will it apply to bootcamp?

  • Status:
    I think I've achieved some form of status through phase 0. If I look back to sprint 1 and where I was in terms of understanding anything, I have definitely gained a whole lot of knowledge since. This knowledge will only become greater in bootcamp. I hope.
  • Certainty:
    I would say my certainty fluctuated throughout phase 0. One moment I would be certain I was capable of getting through this EDA experience, then I would hit a roadblock and all of a sudden I wasn't so sure anymore. One thing I am certain about is that every day I am learning more, and gaining a deeper understanding of what this whole code business is.
  • Autonomy:
    For me, I find there to be a lot I can control in phase 0, and we are given a lot of resources on how to cope with pressure. I have a lot of time before I start bootcamp so I can do the work at whatever pace I feel comfortable with. Maybe this autonomy will diminish once I start bootcamp.
  • Relatedness:
    Everyone I have encountered so far in this experience has been nothing short of fantastic. Everyone is so willing to help each other and share ideas/resources. Whatever apprehension I may of had to begin with was most definitely short lived.
  • Fairness:
    Fairness hasn't been relevant during phase 0. Everyone has been nothing but respectful and selfless. I have no doubt this will continue through bootcamp.

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go?

Honestly, I'm okay with having difficult conversations. Not because I find them enjoyable or anything weird. More because if I have something serious that is bothering me I find it really hard to ignore. I prefer to confront the issue and resolve it asap. Certainly in some cirumstances I agree with the book - sometimes it is better to not raise the issue at all.
