Motivation

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion, and purpose' lens?

I think life looks sparkly through that lens. I think true happiness comes from a combination of all three. If you can take pleasure from life, are passionate about things, and are motivated to achieve a purpose greater than yourself then you're doing pretty well! Unfortunately I don't think it's as easy as that. For me at least. I have a few more lenses I try on from time to time.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, what are your core values? What do you stand for?

My core values:

  • Integrity
  • Respect for myself and others
  • Self-improvement
  • Honesty

What I stand for:

  • Equality
  • Acceptance
  • Peace
  • Fairness
  • Caring for others
  • Caring for the environment

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

I didn't know that resilience could be 'trained', per se. I thought that it was something we might subconsciously learn through life exeperience. So I found it interesting that we could consciously work on our resilience through accessing our inner calm, emotions, and cognition.

The meditation exercise wasn't particuarly pleasant when I focused on my failures. From time to time, these failures crop into my mind naturally. I feel the same emotional and physiological effects in both situations. Embarassment is the main emotion, and I begin to feel hot and uncomfortable. Focussing on success was a bit nicer though. I might try it again one day. We shall see!
